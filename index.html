Projects

Rooftop Photovoltaic System Detection in Heterogeneous Urban and Rural Areas: Application to French Territories.

October 2024 - January 2025 | Polytech Annecy-Chambéry / LOCIE

In this study, we provide an approach for automatically identifying rooftop photovoltaic (PV) installations in various rural and urban environments throughout the French territory. A curated dataset is first created by cleaning and classifying aerial rooftop photos according to whether PV panels are present or not. Several pre-trained convolutional neural network (CNN) models, such as ResNet-18, ResNet-50, ResNeXt-50, EfficientNet, and DenseNet, are trained using this revised dataset. We create classifiers that can reliably differentiate rooftops with and without PV installations by optimizing these models. To assess classification performance, the trained models are then used on a sizable dataset. The models' accuracy and resilience in managing rooftop characteristics and environmental heterogeneity are demonstrated by the experimental results. With an aim to provide an adaptable approach for identifying distributed PV systems.

Efficient Electrical Energy Storage Management: A Case Study of the BEEAU'LAC Project

February 2024 - May 2024 | Polytech Annecy-Chambéry / LOCIE

In this study, we continue the previous work done in the BEEAU LAC project. First, we aim to verify that the OMEGAlpes tool used in the project is actively optimizing the BESS by simulating various energy profiles over a year and ensuring that the model allows the battery to perform in good conditions all year. Secondly, we develop a tool that works on optimizing the BESS by optimizing the charging power and creating BMS-like decisions to time the charge/discharge of the BESS. Lastly, we develop python code that merges with our tool to estimate cycle life .
