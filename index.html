Projects

Rooftop Photovoltaic System Detection in Heterogeneous Urban and Rural Areas: Application to French Territories

Polytech Annecy–Chambéry / LOCIE · October 2024 – January 2025

In this study, we provide an approach for automatically identifying rooftop photovoltaic (PV) installations in various rural and urban environments throughout the French territory. A curated dataset is first created by cleaning and classifying aerial rooftop photos according to whether PV panels are present or not. Several pre-trained convolutional neural network (CNN) models, such as ResNet-18, ResNet-50, ResNeXt-50, EfficientNet, and DenseNet, are trained using this revised dataset. We create classifiers that can reliably differentiate rooftops with and without PV installations by optimizing these models. To assess classification performance, the trained models are then used on a sizable dataset. The models' accuracy and resilience in managing rooftop characteristics and environmental heterogeneity are demonstrated by the experimental results, with the aim of providing an adaptable approach for identifying distributed PV systems.

Efficient Electrical Energy Storage Management: A Case Study of the BEEAU'LAC Project

Polytech Annecy–Chambéry / LOCIE · February 2024 – May 2024

In this study, we continue the previous work done in the BEEAU'LAC project. First, we aim to verify that the OMEGAlpes tool used in the project is actively optimizing the BESS by simulating various energy profiles over a year and ensuring that the model allows the battery to perform in good conditions all year. Secondly, we develop a tool that works on optimizing the BESS by optimizing the charging power and creating BMS-like decisions to time the charge/discharge of the BESS. Lastly, we develop Python code that merges with our tool to estimate cycle life.

Determining the most suitable rooftops for the installation of photovoltaic (PV) systems requires a highly interdisciplinary approach, considering energy, economic, social, and technical criteria. Knowing the precise location of already installed residential PV systems can help urban planners and other stakeholders define deployment scenarios and prioritize rooftops, starting by excluding those that are already equipped. From the perspective of distribution system operators (DSOs), understanding where PV systems are already installed is essential for adapting the grid infrastructure when adding new PV systems.
